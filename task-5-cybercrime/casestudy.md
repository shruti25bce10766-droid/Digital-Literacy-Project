# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a rapidly growing cybercrime in which attackers trick users into transferring money or revealing sensitive banking details.
It usually begins when a fraudster contacts the victim through a phone call, SMS, or messaging apps, pretending to be a bank official, customer support agent, or even a known contact.
The typical process involves several steps. First, the attacker builds trust by creating urgency or offering fake rewards. Next, they send a payment request or a malicious link and instruct the victim to “approve” it,
often claiming it is required to receive money. In some cases, victims are asked to share OTPs or install screen-sharing apps. Once the victim follows these instructions,
the attacker gains access to their account or tricks them into authorizing a payment. Students, elderly individuals, and people unfamiliar with digital payment systems are the most common targets.
The consequences can be severe, including financial loss, stress, and loss of trust in digital platforms.
Such incidents can be prevented by never sharing OTPs, avoiding unknown links, verifying requests independently,
and understanding that receiving money never requires approval. Awareness and cautious behavior are the strongest defenses against UPI fraud.
